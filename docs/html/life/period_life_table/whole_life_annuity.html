Whole life annuity

whole_life_annuity(x, i=0, table='XR', correction=0, beginning=False, freq=1)

Compute the present value of a whole life annuity \(a_x\).

Note

A whole life annuity consists of a series of 1 unit payments for as long as an individual aged \(x\) is alive.

If the payment frequency is annual, the present value is given by

\[a_x = \sum_{k \geq 1} {}_kE_x = \frac{p_x}{1+i} + \frac{_2p_x}{(1+i)^2} + ...\]

in the case of payments at the end of the year, or

\[\ddot{a}_x = \sum_{k \geq 0} {}_kE_x = 1 + \frac{p_x}{1+i} + \frac{_2p_x}{(1+i)^2} + ...\]

in the case of payments at the beginning of the year.

If the payment frequency is split (e.g., monthly payments), the present value is given by

\[a_x^{(m)} = \sum_{k \geq 1} {}_{\frac{k}{m}}E_x \qquad \text{and} \qquad \ddot{a}_x^{(m)} = \sum_{k \geq 0} {}_{\frac{k}{m}}E_x\]

where \(m\) is the number of payments per year (e.g., \(m=12\) for monthly payments or \(m=4\) for quarterly payments).

Finally, we have the following relations:

\[\ddot{a}_x = 1 + a_x \qquad \text{and} \qquad a_x = {}_na_x + {}_nE_x \cdot a_{x+n}.\]
Parameters

  • x (numeric) – Age.

  • i (numeric) – Guaranteed interest rate.

  • table (string) – Life table to use.

  • correction (numeric) – Age correction. The correction can be positive (age increase) or negative (age reduction). The default assumes no correction.

  • beginning (bool) – Indicate whether the payments occur at the beginning of the period. The default assumes end of period payments.

  • freq (numeric) – Payment frequency: number of payments per year. The default assumes annual payment.

Returns

Present value of the whole life annuity.

Return type

numeric (non-negative)

Examples

Compute \(a_{50}\) and \(\ddot{a}_{50}\) according to the MR table with \(i=2\%\) (the annuity is paid on a yearly basis):

>>> whole_life_annuity(50, 0.02, "MR")
21.68
>>> whole_life_annuity(50, 0.02, "MR", TRUE)
22.68

Compute \(a_{54.5}^{(4)}\) according to the FR table with \(i=2\%\) (the annuity is paid on a quarterly basis):

>>> whole_life_annuity(54.5, 0.02, "FR",,,4)
89.34

Compute \(\ddot{a}_{80}^{(12)}\) according to the XR table with \(i=2\%\) and an age correction of -3 years (the annuity is paid on a monthly basis):

>>> whole_life_annuity(80, 0.02, "XR", -3, TRUE, 12)
118.95
